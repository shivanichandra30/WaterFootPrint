What is Water FootPrint?

a measure of the amount of fresh water utilized
in the production or supply of the goods and services used by a particular person or group.






Indoor

Direct Water FootPrint

This refers to the amount of water an individual consumed for household activities such as cooking,cleaning,drinking and other immediate needs.

Outdoor

Direct Water FootPrint

This refers to the amount of water an individual consumes for activities outside one's house such as gardening,car wash,swimming pool maintenance,etc.

Virtual

Indirect Water FootPrint

Virtual water is the water “hidden” in the products, services and processes people buy and use every day. Virtual water often goes unseen by the end-user of a product or service.

Types of Water FootPrints



Blue

The "blue water footprint" refers to the amount of freshwater that is consumed in the production process of a particular product or service. It specifically accounts for the volume of surface and groundwater used during the various stages of production, including the growth of raw materials, manufacturing, and processing.

Green

The "green water footprint" refers to the amount of water which comes from rain and humidity that is stored in the soil and used by plants during their growth process. It represents the volume of precipitation that is effectively utilized by vegetation for photosynthesis and other biological processes.

Grey

The "grey water footprint" refers to the amount of freshwater that is required to dilute pollutants or contaminants in wastewater to a level that it can be safely released back into the environment without causing harm. It is a measure of the environmental impact associated with the release of pollutants.
